1) Leopard's height, weight and lifespan

Leopards grow from 92 to 190 centimeters (3 to 6.2 feet ) long. Their tail adds another 64 to 99 cm (25 to 39 inches) to their length. Males and females vary in weight. Females typically weigh 21 to 60 kilograms (46 to 132 pounds) and males usually weigh around 36 to 75 kg. (80 to 165 pounds), The lifespan of a leopard is between 12 and 17 years in the wild, and up to 23 years in captivity.

2) Leopard Behavior is Secretive and Territorial

Adult leopards are solitary animals and only spend time with others to mate and to raise their young. Each leopard spends most of its life in its own territory. And to warn other leopards to stay away, they leave scratches on trees, and urine scent marks around their areas. They also rub their faces against vegetation along other pathways and territory boundaries. In doing so, they transfer secretions from the cheek glands, which can also carry olfactory messages.
